Music Theory

Concepts to understand, not just memorize.

```
Foundations
The CAGED System
The guitar neck repeats 5 chord shapes — C, A, G, E, D — up the neck. Every major chord can be played in all 5 positions. Once you know where your root note falls within each shape, you can play any chord anywhere.

Harmony
Circle of Fifths
Moving clockwise adds one sharp, counterclockwise adds one flat. Keys a fifth apart share almost all the same notes — that's why chord progressions like I → IV → V sound so natural. In G major: G → C → D.
Scales
Modes from the Major Scale
Start the major scale on a different degree and you get a different mode. Dorian (2nd degree) sounds minor but with a raised 6th — jazzier and less dark than natural minor. Mixolydian (5th) is the blues-rock workhorse — major with a flat 7.
Rhythm
Subdivisions
Quarter notes divide into 8ths (1-and), which divide into 16ths (1-e-and-a). Most rock and folk sits in 8ths. Triplets split a beat into 3 — the shuffle feel comes from playing only the 1st and 3rd of each triplet.
```
